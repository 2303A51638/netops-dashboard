Paper Abstract
Research Focus
This paper examines the convergence of DevOps methodologies with 5G network infrastructure, focusing on Cloud-Native Network Functions (CNFs), Software-Defined Networking (SDN), and Network Function Virtualization (NFV). It draws on standards from 3GPP, ETSI, and ITU-T alongside industry white papers from Ericsson and Cisco.
Central Argument
Legacy telecom architectures require fundamental transformation to meet 5G service requirements — particularly ultra-low latency (uRLLC) and massive IoT throughput (mMTC). The paper advocates for AI-driven network automation (AI4NetOps) and continuous delivery pipelines adapted for virtualized network functions.
Projected Impact
Cisco's 2023 Internet Report projects multi-fold IP traffic growth through 2023. Combined with 3GPP TS 22.261 service requirements, this motivates automated, cloud-native network management as the dominant paradigm for next-generation carrier networks.

Research Gaps & Recommendations
Critical observations and suggested directions for future work
Identified Gaps
G1 · Limited Peer-Reviewed Empirical Evidence
Only one IEEE journal article (Ref 6 — Xu et al.) constitutes peer-reviewed academic research. The remaining 7 references are standards, white papers, or a practitioner book. Future work should include empirical case studies from live 5G deployments measuring DevOps KPIs (MTTR, deployment frequency, change failure rate).
G2 · Vendor Neutrality Concern
Two industry references (Ericsson Ref 4, Cisco Ref 7) originate from vendors with commercial interest in 5G infrastructure. Independent third-party validation or academic replication of their claims would strengthen the paper's objectivity.
G3 · Security & Compliance Absent
No reference addresses DevSecOps, zero-trust architectures, or compliance automation in telecom pipelines — a critical omission for carrier-grade deployments subject to GSMA guidelines and national telecom regulations.
G4 · Open-Source Toolchain Coverage Missing
The paper does not reference ONAP, OSM, or OpenStack Tacker — key open-source platforms for NFV orchestration. These represent significant practical deployment contexts for the DevOps approaches described.
G5 · Post-2023 Literature Needed
The Cisco report (Ref 7) is the most recent at 2023. Literature on O-RAN DevOps integration, LLM-assisted network operations, and 5G Advanced (Release 18/19) management requirements is absent and would significantly strengthen contemporary relevance.
